 Getting Over It


1. The game involves controlling a man named Diogenes, who sits in a cauldron and uses a large hammer to move. The game's physics engine plays a crucial role in how players navigate the environment.
2. The game is notoriously difficult, with many obstacles that can easily cause players to lose progress. There are no checkpoints, and falling can set players back significantly.
3. he game has a simple aesthetic, with a focus on the protagonist and the landscape rather than detailed graphics. This minimalism extends to the gameplay mechanics, which are easy to understand but hard to master.
4. Bennett Foddy, the creator, provides voice-over narration throughout the game, offering philosophical insights, reflections on failure, and commentary on the nature of the game and the player's experience.
5. The game contains various Easter eggs and references to other games, literature, and media. These often add a layer of humor or meta-commentary...
